Frequently Asked Questions about Hackathon at Saegis Code Pulse

The Hackathon competition is a key event at Saegis Code Pulse, organized under the tertiary category to enhance the software development capabilities of undergraduates from state and private universities/institutes in Sri Lanka. The competition consists of two rounds, with cash prizes for the top three groups.

All contestants in a group must be undergraduates from a state university or any higher education institute in Sri Lanka. Group members should be from the same university or institute, and each group should consist of four members.

The competition consists of two rounds. All eligible groups participate in the first round. The top 10 groups from the first round qualify for the final round where they compete for the top three cash prizes.

No, team members who have registered and participated in the first round are the only ones allowed to participate in the final round. Replacement of team members is not allowed.

Competitors have the freedom to select any technologies they prefer for their projects.

Each group should consist of four members.

The Initial Round takes place online. Participating groups are required to prepare and submit a video about a software system. Detailed guidelines will be provided after the application closing date.

Judges will evaluate the submitted videos to select the best 10 groups that will proceed to the Final Round.

The Final Round will take place on campus and will be a 10-12 hour Hackathon event. Participating groups will work on solving a specific problem during this time.

Judges will evaluate the solutions and performances of the participating groups during the Final Round to select the top 3 groups for the awards.

Frequently Asked Questions about DESIGNATHON at Saegis Code Pulse

DESIGNATHON is one of the key competitions at Saegis Code Pulse, focusing on enhancing the UI/UX design capabilities of undergraduates from state and private universities/institutes in Sri Lanka.

DESIGNATHON is organized in two rounds. All eligible groups qualify for the first round. The top 10 groups from the first round proceed to the final round, where the best three groups will be awarded cash prizes.

Each team should consist of four members.

All contestants of a group must be undergraduates from a state university or any other higher education institute in Sri Lanka. All group members should be from the same university or institute.

Competitors have the freedom to select their preferred technologies.

Once registered, team members cannot be replaced with new members. Only team members who registered and participated in the first round are allowed to participate in the final round.

The Initial Round takes place online. Participating groups are required to prepare and submit a video about a software system. Detailed guidelines will be provided after the application closing date.

Judges will evaluate the submitted videos to select the top 10 groups that will proceed to the Final Round.

The Final Round will take place on campus and will be a 10-12 hour DESIGNATHON competition. Participating groups will work on solving a specific UI/UX design problem during this time.

Judges will evaluate the solutions and performances of the participating groups during the Final Round to select the top 3 groups for the awards.
