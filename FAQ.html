Frequently Asked Questions about Hackathon at Saegis Code Pulse

The Hackathon competition is a key event at Saegis Code Pulse, organized exclusively for Saegis campus students to enhance their software development skills. The competition consists of a single round held on campus, with cash prizes for the top three groups.

The Hackathon competition is open only to Saegis campus students. Each group should consist of four members from the same campus.

The Hackathon competition consists of a single round held on campus. All eligible groups participate in this round, and the top three groups will be awarded cash prizes.

No, team members who have registered and participated in the Hackathon round are the only ones allowed to participate. Replacement of team members is not allowed.

Competitors have the freedom to select any technologies they prefer for their projects.

Each group should consist of four members.

The Hackathon round will take place on campus and will be a 10-12 hour event. Participating groups will work on solving a specific problem during this time.

Judges will evaluate the solutions and performances of the participating groups during the Hackathon round to select the top 3 groups for the awards.

Frequently Asked Questions about DESIGNATHON at Saegis Code Pulse

DESIGNATHON is one of the key competitions at Saegis Code Pulse, focusing on enhancing the UI/UX design capabilities of Saegis campus students.

DESIGNATHON is organized as a single round. All eligible Saegis campus student groups participate in this round, where the top three groups will be awarded cash prizes.

Each team should consist of four members.

DESIGNATHON is open to all Saegis campus students. All group members should be from the same campus.

Competitors have the freedom to select their preferred technologies.

Once registered, team members cannot be replaced with new members. Only team members who registered and participated in the first round are allowed to participate in the final round.

The Initial Round takes place on campus. Participating groups will be required to prepare and present a UI/UX design concept. Detailed guidelines will be provided after the application closing date.

Judges will evaluate the submitted UI/UX design concepts to select the top 3 groups for the awards.
